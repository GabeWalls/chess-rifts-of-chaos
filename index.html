üìñ Chess: Rifts of Chaos - Game Rules

üéØ Game Objective

The goal is to checkmate your opponent's king, just like traditional chess. However, mystical rifts scattered across the board will trigger powerful and unpredictable effects that can dramatically change the game!

‚öôÔ∏è Setup Phase

  1. Place 4 Rifts: Both players place 4 rifts on the board (rows 3-6 only).
  2. Placement Rules: No two rifts can share the same row or column.
  3. Random Option: Use "Generate Random Rifts" for quick setup.
  4. Multiplayer: The host controls when the game starts after rifts are placed.

‚ôüÔ∏è Basic Gameplay

  • Standard Chess Rules: All pieces move according to traditional chess rules.
  • Taking Turns: White moves first, then players alternate turns.
  • Capturing: Captured pieces are removed and stored (some rift effects use them!).
  • Winning: Checkmate the enemy king or force your opponent to resign.

üåÄ Rift Mechanics

When any piece lands on a rift, that rift is activated:

  1. The rift disappears from the board (one-time use).
  2. Roll a D20 (20-sided die) to determine the effect.
  3. Apply the effect immediately (some effects may require choices).
  4. Continue playing based on the effect's outcome.

üí° Strategy Tip: Plan your moves carefully! Landing on rifts can be beneficial or harmful depending on the roll and game state.

üé≤ Rift Effects (D20 Results)

1 - Necromancer's Trap: Remove your piece. Place one of your opponent's captured pieces onto the rift. Requires: Opponent must have captured pieces.
2 - Archer's Trick Shot: Remove an enemy piece within 3 squares (any direction) from the rift.
3 - Sandworm: Removes all pieces (yours and opponent's) within 1 square of the rift.
4 - Honorable Sacrifice: The piece that activated the rift is removed from the board.
5 - Demotion: The activating piece (if not a pawn) is replaced with a captured pawn from your reserves. Requires: Must have captured pawns, activating piece cannot be a pawn.
6 - Foot Soldier's Gambit: The activating piece immediately moves again (same piece, not an extra turn).
7 - Famine: Each player loses one captured piece from their reserves.
8 - Earthquake: Randomly removes 3 pieces from anywhere on the board.
9 - Sandstorm: Field Effect. All pieces can now move like knights (L-shape jumps). Effect remains until another field effect is activated. Requires: Both players must have moved at least once.
10 - Dragon's Breath: Remove the first enemy piece in a chosen direction (N, S, E, W, NE, NW, SE, SW) from the rift.
11 - Jack Frost's Mischief: Roll another D20. On 1-10: freeze the activating piece for 1 turn. On 11-20: no effect.
12 - Portal in the Rift: Teleport the activating piece to any other unactivated rift on the board.
13 - Catapult Roulette: Roll 2D8 (two 8-sided dice). The numbers determine a random coordinate. Remove the piece at that location (if any).
14 - Conqueror's Tale: Your king gains the ability to move twice in one turn (only the king, total of 2 moves, then turn ends).
15 - Promotion: If a pawn activated this, promote it to any piece (Queen, Rook, Bishop, Knight). Otherwise, no effect.
16 - Time Distortion/Stasis: Field Effect. All pieces within 2 squares of the rift are frozen and cannot move. Effect remains until another field effect is activated.
17 - Divine Intervention: The activating piece is returned to its starting position.
18 - Fairy Fountain: If a pawn activated this, it gains special movement: forward 2 spaces, plus diagonal 1 space. Requires: Activating piece must be a pawn.
19 - Eerie Fog's Turmoil: Field Effect. At the start of your NEXT turn, roll D20: 3-20 = play normally. 1-2 = skip that turn.
20 - Spring of Revival: Choose one of your captured pieces and place it on any valid starting square (back row). Requires: Must have captured pieces.

üåç Field Effects

Some rift effects create field effects that persist until:

  • Another field effect is rolled (replaces the current one)
  • The same field effect is rolled again (cancels itself)

Active Field Effects: Sandstorm (#9), Time Distortion/Stasis (#16), Eerie Fog's Turmoil (#19)

üìä Current field effects are displayed in the "Active Field Effects" section.
